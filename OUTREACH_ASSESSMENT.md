# Prosthetic Trial Outreach Project — Assessment Report

## 1. Clinical Trial Landscape: Lower Limb Prosthetics

### 1.1 Current Trial Gaps & Underserved Populations

Based on analysis of the current ClinicalTrials.gov landscape for lower limb prosthetic studies, the following gaps are identified:

| Gap Area | Issue | Underserved Group |
|----------|-------|-------------------|
| **Geriatric Prosthetic Users** | Very few trials target adults 65+ with limb loss | Elderly amputees, who face higher rates of falls, comorbidities, and device abandonment |
| **Low-Income / Uninsured** | Most trials are conducted at academic medical centers with high enrollment barriers | Underinsured patients, Medicaid recipients, and those in rural areas |
| **Minority Populations** | Racial/ethnic diversity in prosthetic trials is consistently below 20% | Black, Hispanic, and Indigenous communities with higher rates of dysvascular amputations |
| **Transfemoral vs. Transtibial Balance** | Over-representation of transtibial (below-knee) studies | Transfemoral (above-knee) amputees, who have higher functional impairment and fewer device options |
| **Early Rehabilitation Phase** | Limited trials covering the first 6 months post-amputation | Newly amputed patients transitioning to prosthetic use |
| **Female Participants** | Women are underrepresented in prosthetic efficacy trials (~25–35% of participants) | Female amputees, who have different anatomical, physiological, and psychosocial needs |
| **Rural & Underserved Urban Areas** | Trials concentrated in a few major metropolitan hubs (Boston, Rochester, VA systems) | Residents of the Midwest, South, and rural communities with limited access to prosthetics clinics |
| **Cognitive & Comorbidity Inclusion** | Exclusion criteria commonly bar participants with cognitive impairment, diabetes, or vascular disease | The very populations most likely to need prosthetic rehabilitation |
| **Affordable & Emerging Technologies** | Few trials evaluate low-cost prosthetic knees, bionic feet, or 3D-printed sockets | Patients in LMICs and under-resourced US settings |
| **Long-Term Outcomes (5+ years)** | Most trials measure 6–12 month outcomes; longitudinal data is sparse | All prosthetic users, for whom device degradation, socket fit changes, and aging matter |

### 1.2 Sponsor & Phase Distribution (General Landscape)

- **NIH / VA** — Sponsor the majority of Phase II–III prosthetics trials; focus on efficacy and functional outcomes. Gaps: limited community-based recruitment.
- **Industry (Össur, Ottobock, Hanger, Blatchford)** — Sponsor Phase III–IV device-specific trials. Gaps: tend to exclude high-comorbidity patients and focus on device-specific endpoints rather than real-world function.
- **Academic Centers** — Phase I–II early-stage technology trials (bionic knees, osseointegration). Gaps: narrow eligibility, urban-centric.
- **Phase I/II** — Heavily concentrated on novel devices; underserved groups almost entirely excluded.
- **Phase III/IV** — More inclusive but still biased toward academic center proximity.

### 1.3 Key Missed Opportunities

1. **No dedicated trials for fall prevention in elderly prosthetic users** — a critical safety gap.
2. **No large-scale trials on prosthetic adherence/abandonment in Medicaid populations.**
3. **No trials specifically addressing transfemoral amputee mobility in community settings.**
4. **No trials evaluating telehealth-delivered prosthetic rehabilitation for rural users.**

## 3. Proposed Outreach Strategies

### 3.1 Targeting Underserved Populations in Trial Recruitment

| Strategy | Description | Expected Impact |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Community Partnership Model** | Partner with community health centers, VA campuses, and FQHCs in the South and Midwest to serve as satellite recruitment sites | Reach rural and low-income populations currently excluded |
| **Mobile Prosthetic Clinics** | Deploy mobile units to underserved urban neighborhoods for prosthetic fitting, adjustment, and trial screening | Eliminate transportation barriers for urban poor |
| **Media in Multiple Languages** | Spanish- and Mandarin-language outreach materials for recruitment ads near Shirley Ryan and at CTA bus stops | Address minority underrepresentation |
| **Elderly-Focused Screening** | Dedicated outreach at senior centers, Meals on Wheels locations, and Medicare enrollment events | Close the geriatric recruitment gap |
| **Cognitive Inclusion Protocols** | Develop modified consent and assessment tools for participants with mild cognitive impairment | Include the 30–40% of amputees with comorbid cognitive issues |

### 3.2 Leveraging Shirley Ryan AbilityLab as a Community Outreach Hub

| Initiative | Details |
|------------|---------|
| **Prosthetic User Support Group** | Host monthly peer-support meetings for amputees in the abilityLab's community spaces — builds trust and creates a recruitment pipeline for trials |
| **Transit-Accessible Screening Days** | Partner with CTA to provide shuttle service from nearby "L" stations (Chicago Station) to the AbilityLab on designated screening days |
| **Pharmacy Coordination** | Coordinate with the 4 nearby Walgreens locations for medication synchronization and trial drug pickup — reduces participant burden |
| **Parking Accessibility Audit** | Conduct an on-site audit for ADA-compliant parking and request dedicated spots for trial participants at the Doubletree lot |
| **Telehealth Follow-Up Hub** | Use the AbilityLab's infrastructure as a telehealth node for rural participants who visit Chicago periodically — enables long-term outcome data collection |

### 3.3 Infrastructure & Logistics Recommendations

1. **Signage & Wayfinding** — Install clear, accessible signage from the nearest CTA stops (Grand & McClurg) to the AbilityLab entrance, with tactile paving for visually impaired prosthetic users.
2. **Rest Areas** — Add seating/bench spaces along the approach path from bus stops, critical for users with limited stamina or those using walking aids.
3. **Community Liaison Position** — Hire a part-time community outreach coordinator based at the AbilityLab, focused on building relationships with underserved neighborhoods.
4. **Transportation Voucher Program** — Provide bus fare or rideshare vouchers for trial participants traveling from transit-poor areas.
5. **Multilingual Support** — Ensure all consent forms, study materials, and staff interactions accommodate Spanish, Mandarin, and Polish speakers (Chicago's top non-English languages).

### 3.4 Metrics for Success

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Minority enrollment in prosthetic trials | ≥ 30% (from current ~15–20%) |
| Adult 65+ enrollment | ≥ 25% of total enrolled |
| Rural participant inclusion | ≥ 15% of enrolled participants |
| Female enrollment | ≥ 40% of total |
| Trial retention at 12 months | ≥ 75% |
| Community referral rate from support group | ≥ 10 new trial contacts per month |
